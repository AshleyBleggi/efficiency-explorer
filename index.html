Context:

In May 2016, Governor Brown issued Executive Order B-37-16. The EO calls for the development of water use targets customized to the unique conditions of each urban water agency as part of a new, permanent efficiency framework.

Standards for efficiency are being developed by the Department of Water Resources and the Water Board in consultation with urban water suppliers and other stakeholder groups.

At present, the CaDC Efficiency Explorer tool focuses on the residential component of an agency's efficiency target. This component will vary according to:

Location-Specific Factors Policy Decisions
Population Reasonable gallons per capita per day water production (GPCD)
Residential landscaped area The natural water use characteristics of appropriate landscape (ET Adjustment Factor)
Reference evapotranspiration

These elements can be combined in the following way to give a customized residential target for any period of time:

Residential Indoor Budget = GPCD x Population x Number of days in period
Residential Outdoor Budget = Sum over days in period( ET Adjustment Factor x Daily reference evapotranspiration x Unit Conversion Factor x Residential landscaped area )
Residential Target = Indoor budget + Outdoor budget

Instructions:

The CaDC Efficiency Explorer is a planning tool for local water managers and the California water community. By setting values for GPCD and ET Adjustment Factor, users can investigate how different standards for residential water use affect their agencies over different periods of time.

  • Use the Scenario Builder panel to:
    • Set scenario parameters
      • Date Range - by clicking and dragging the slider knobs
      • GPCD and ET Adjustment Factor - by typing values in the associated forms and pressing enter
    • View results for current scenario for selected Supplier
      • Residential Target - the target residential production calculated using the selected indoor and outdoor water efficiency standards
      • Residential Production - the total production reported by the supplier multiplied by the percentage that went to residential customers
      • Efficiency - the number of acre-feet (and percentage) over or within target. Negative percentages represent percentage within target
      • Time Series Graph - the historical relationship between target production and actual production
  • Use the map panel to:
    • Select suppliers
    • View geographic context
    • View the Big Picture
      • SBx7-7 2020 Residential Target - the amount of water in acre-feet for the previous twelve months for suppliers with SBx7-7 targets. The Residential Target is calculated as the percent residential reported in the supplier report multiplied by the total SBx7-7 target for that supplier
      • Total Residential Target - the sum of all residential targets for the same suppliers as the SBx7 2020 Residential Target for the previous twelve months
      • Total Residential Production - the sum of all residential production for the same suppliers as the SBx7-7 2020 Residential Target for the previous twelve months
