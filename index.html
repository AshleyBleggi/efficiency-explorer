Context:

In May 2016, Governor Brown issued Executive Order B-37-16. The EO calls for the development of water use targets customized to the unique conditions of each urban water agency as part of a new, permanent efficiency framework.

Standards for efficiency are being developed by the Department of Water Resources and the Water Board in consultation with urban water suppliers and other stakeholder groups.

At present, the Efficiency Explorer tool the CaDC has developed focuses on the residential usage component of an agency's efficiency target. This component will vary according to:

Location-Specific Factors Policy Decisions
Population Reasonable gallons per capita per day usage (GPCD)
Landscaped area The natural water use characteristics of appropriate landscape (ET Adjustment Factor)
Reference evapotranspiration

These elements can be combined in the following way to give a customized residential usage target for any period of time:

Indoor budget = GPCD x Population x Number of days in period
Outdoor budget = Sum over days in period(ET Adjustment Factor x Daily reference evapotranspiration x Unit Conversion Factor x Landscaped area)
Target = Indoor budget + Outdoor budget

Instructions:

This tool aims to serve water managers by both providing an intuitive platform for exploring the new efficiency targets, and by enabling instantaneous access to data for different water use policy scenarios. By setting values for GPCD and ET Adjustment Factor, managers can use this tool to investigate how different standards for residential water use affect their agencies over different periods of time.

  • Use the Scenario Builder panel to:
    • Set scenario parameters
      • Date Range, by clicking and dragging the slider knobs
      • GPCD and ET Adjustment Factor, by typing values in the associated forms and pressing enter
    • View results for selected supplier
      • Residential usage target
      • Number of acre-feet (and percentage) over or within target for current scenario. Negative percentages represent percentage within target
      • Historical relationship between target usage and actual usage
  • Use the Map panel to:
    • Select suppliers
    • View statewide implications
