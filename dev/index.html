Context:

In May 2016, Governor Brown issued Executive Order B-37-16. The EO calls for the development of water use targets customized to the unique conditions of each urban water agency as part of a new, permanent efficiency framework.

Standards for efficiency are being developed by the Department of Water Resources and the Water Board in consultation with urban water suppliers and other stakeholder groups.

This new water use framework shall be publicly proposed by January 10, 2017.

At present, the efficiency benchmarking tools the CaDC has developed focus on the residential usage component of an agency's efficiency target. This component will vary according to:

District-Specific Factors Policy Decisions
Population Reasonable gallons per capita per day usage (GPCD)
Irrigable area The natural water use characteristics of appropriate landscape (Plant Factor)
Evapotranspiration

Instructions:

This tool aims to serve water managers by both providing an intuitive platform for exploring the new efficiency targets, and by enabling instantaneous access to data for different water use policy scenarios.

The Agency-Wide Efficiency Explorer addresses benchmarking scenarios at the state-level. By setting values for GPCD (gallons per capita per day) and Plant Factor, managers can use this tool to investigate how different standards for residential water use affect their agencies over different periods of time.

The Neighborhood-Level Efficiency Explorer uses the same scenario building features described above to explore water use efficiency across census blocks within a single district. Managers can use this tool to identify communities that are potentially historically under-preforming and allocate conservation intervention resources accordingly.

Specifically, each tool consists of a map for selecting an agency or census block, and Scenario Builder panel for (1) selecting Date Range, GPCD, and Plant Factor; and (2) viewing results (residential usage target, number of acre-feet over or under target for current scenario, and a time series graph for comparing target usage to actual usage over time).

Use the navigation bar links above to scroll between tools. Please set your browser window to full screen for best experience.
